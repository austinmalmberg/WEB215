Gear

Just like being outdoors, when it comes to the cost of equipment, the sky is the limit. As a rule of thumb, weight is indrectly proportional to cost, or rather, the more expensive it is, the lighter it will be. As you might expect, expensive gear also tends to last longer as well.

While picking out my gear, I wanted to balance price and quality. Light gear is great, but I'm in decent shape so this was low on my totem pole of concerns.

Tent: MSR Hubba Hubba NX 2

Deal of a lifetime. Retails for $450. REI garage sale: $81

Backpack: Teton Sports Expolorer 4000

Was invited by my buddy to do an overnight at South Mountain and didn't have a backpack. I did have Amazon Prime though. One of my camping first purchases. Amazon: $55

Sleeping Bag: REI Trail Pod 30

Invaluable in the fall or a warm winter. My first REI purchase: $44

Sleeping Pad: REI Trekker Self-Inflating

Not really meant for hiking because it's not all that light and can't be compressed. But it is comfy...and I caught it on sale. REI: $48

Stove: MSR PocketRocket 2

Had a 20% off coupon from REI and couldn't resist. REI: $36

Cookset: Snow Peak Personal Cooker 3 Cookset

Bought with the stove. They aren't titanium, but they're a helluvalot cheaper. REI: $24

Jacket: REI Magma 850 Down Hoodie

Unbelievable warm for its weight. Bought on sale. REI: $108

Waterproof Jacket: Outdoor Research Helium II

Packable. Too make rainy days more bearable. Bought on sale. REI: $89
